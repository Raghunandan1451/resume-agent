## FIELD RULES

roleTitle
- Find the EXACT job title as written in the JD. Look for it near words like
  "Job Title:", "Role:", "hiring a", "looking for a", "position of".
- Copy it character-for-character. Do not paraphrase, generalize, or infer.
- If the JD says "ReactJS Developer" write "ReactJS Developer" — not "Frontend Developer",
  not "React Developer", not "Frontend Engineer".
- Only infer if no explicit title exists anywhere in the JD.
- Example: "We are looking for a skilled ReactJS Developer" → roleTitle is "ReactJS Developer"
- Example: "Job Title: Full Stack Engineer" → roleTitle is "Full Stack Engineer"

experienceLevel
- Use "junior", "mid", "senior", "lead", "manager", "intern", or "".
- Base this on stated years, seniority words, scope, and responsibility.
- Do not infer seniority from technology names alone.

requiredSkills
- Include must-have skills, qualifications, technologies, tools, and domain skills.
- Use short names such as "React", "TypeScript", "REST APIs", "Redux".
- Exclude soft skills unless they are central requirements.
- Maximum 12 items.

preferredSkills
- Include nice-to-have skills explicitly marked as preferred, bonus, plus, desirable, or equivalent.
- Do not duplicate requiredSkills.
- Maximum 10 items.

coreTechnologies
- List specific languages, frameworks, libraries, platforms, databases, tooling, and protocols mentioned anywhere in the JD.
- Include both required and preferred technologies.
- Do not include broad responsibilities such as "frontend development".
- Maximum 15 items.

responsibilities
- List concrete job duties from the JD.
- Use concise noun or verb phrases such as "build reusable UI components", "integrate REST APIs", "maintain CI/CD pipelines".
- Maximum 8 items.

focusAreas
- List higher-level role themes such as "frontend architecture", "state management", "performance optimization", "data integrity", "real-time features".
- Maximum 8 items.

industryKeywords
- List domain, product, compliance, business, or workflow terms that matter for tailoring.
- Examples: "healthcare", "fintech", "SaaS", "CRM", "analytics", "enterprise", "workflow automation".
- Maximum 10 items.

tone
- Use "formal", "casual", "startup", "enterprise", "technical", or "".
- Reflect the JD's writing style, not the company brand by assumption.
